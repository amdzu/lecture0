What is Lukulab?

Lukulab is a reading intervention that helps struggling and dyslexic readers become masterful ones and empowers parents to get them there.

  • AI-tutoring

    Our reading clinic provides intensive, AI-powered tutoring sessions that children can complete with any adult instead of a private tutor, making personalized instruction accessible anywhere and affordable to all.

  • Adaptive Learning System

    By continually tailoring lessons to the specific needs and talents of each child, our adaptive learning system gives children the skills they need to read for life.

Our Approach

  • Comprehensive

    Lukulab’s tutoring sessions teach children to pull written and spoken language apart into its smallest parts, put them back together, and make sense of words, sentences, and whole stories. Children learn to guess less at new words, read smoothly, and find meaning in what they read.

    Explicit instruction meets students where they are in learning the elements of reading, including phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension. Our library provides stories that match students’ reading level and age, respecting struggling readers as capable thinkers.

Questions and Answers

What exactly does an adult do during tutoring sessions?

An adult listens to how the child completes oral exercises, tells the computer if she got the oral exercise right, and helps her with new vocabulary and unfamiliar words encountered in texts. Like any good children’s programming, our activities are often as novel and engaging for adults as they are for children. Directions are clear and easy to follow.

What age and reading levels is it designed for?

LukuLab is designed for children aged 7-11 who have started to learn to read and encountered difficulty. It is ideal both for children with a language-based learning disability, including dyslexia, and for children who have no diagnosis, but for whom reading is hard going.

What kind of reading problems does your program address?

As reading specialists, linguists, teachers, and tutors, we have broken down the big difficulties that struggling readers encounter into dozens of micro-difficulties, each of which is addressed through targeted activities. These big difficulties include, for example, wildly guessing at words, trouble sounding out words, getting similar-sounding words or similar-looking letters confused, slow and awkward reading, and poor reading comprehension.

Do you follow a certain teaching methodology?

All effective reading interventions draw on principles of structured, code-based approaches to literacy, and ours is no exception. LukuLab uses both established and original methodologies to teach each child to break the reading code.

What if my child can’t sit still for thirty minutes?

Tutoring sessions are designed so that children work for only ten minutes at a time. We recommend that children complete twelve of these ten-minute blocks per week (typically broken down into three blocks a day, four days a week). Breaks between blocks can be a few minutes, a whole day, or whatever stretch suits your child’s needs.
